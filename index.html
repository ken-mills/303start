A foundation for creating an excellent company is an investment in people. Knowledge, skill, and attitude are important competencies to develop and monitor. team303 does that but organizations often forget to invest in and nurture the relationships between people, between supervisors and employees, between executives and managers, or between coaches and players. The team303 design was based on this core principle. team303 provides tools to develop strong, transparent, mentoring relationships between people.

Organizations everywhere including many in the Fortune 500 have decided to trash their bureaucratic, paper based, time sucking, performance managements systems. Why? Organizations are moving too fast. These systems were designed for a different era. Many organizations are now simply asking their managers and supervisors to meet with staff more frequently, communicate more, and create more time for mentoring but with what tools? How does your CEO know that this is actually happening? team303 provides the toolbox to help supervisors engage with their team. team303 provides CEOs with a window into how their employees are interacting light, fun, non-threatening way.

team303 provides an optional player / coach metaphor to take the work out of the language used to develop team players by engaging and motivating them in a different way.

team303 has optional components that allow your organization to select and customize an employee engagement system that fits your culture. You decide which components and features are right for your people.

team303 components and features

pre-game the pre-game provides coaches and players with a template agenda for meeting at the beginning of the year. a set of simple questions that focus on how both can work together more effectively. you can customize the template for your organization. players capture notes to prepare for the meeting.
winning a simple way to capture player goals. wse this tool to promote transparent communication and discussion. pre-set character limits and instructions that you can customize. no paper forms.
training camp identify and record player training goals to help your organization build an inventory of knowledge and skills
huddle quickly document results of frequent discussions. how are we working together? how are you progressing toward your organizational and training goals? how can I help? what is in your way? how is your innovation progressing? don't wait until the end of the year to find out when its too late.
ideas innovative ideas can be nurtured in more than just a suggestion box. players like to know their ideas are being heard and shared. players need to understand the steps required to make new product ideas a reality. this tool provides a simple framework for vetting and supporting ideas within your organization.
the wave reward your employees for engaging with team303. the wave generates a stream of player activity like a log file and a badge system that promotes engagement, communication, and player development. visualize in real-time how players are engaging.
scorecard you just killed your old performance management system but your ceo still wants information, who is talking, who is growing. capture an inventory of skills, skills being developed. see who is actively engaging.

about 303start

303start and team303 were created by me, Ken Mills. I am a software developer and former information technology executive. Organization's are struggling to find a performance management system that fit today's employees. Many organizations are using systems that were designed and developed many years ago for a different era. The culture and tools used by employees at work are vastly different yet the way many organizations engage and motivate employees has not changed. I am suggesting that there is a better way. Through the team303 application and the information on this site, I hope to share what I have learned throughout my career as a manager. Some people write books. I write software.

The ideas for team303 originated in 2009. The hope was to create a more effective coaching resource for supervisors and managers. They need a system that provides time to working with staff and less time documenting. They need a resource ( a framework ) to guide them along the way. To build strong individual relationships, mentor and build great teams, they need simple way to document there conversations so executives have the information they need to grow their organizations.
